Photosensitivity Warning

This application produces rapidly flashing lights designed to simulate the Brion Gysin Dream Machine — a stroboscopic device for visual brainwave entrainment.

Do not use if you:

  • Have epilepsy or a history of seizures
  • Have a family history of epilepsy
  • Are prone to photosensitive reactions

For best results: dark room, eyes closed, face toward screen. Headphones or speakers for audio.

How It Works

The Dream Machine uses flickering light to synchronize your brainwaves through photic driving — your visual cortex locks onto the flicker frequency and generates geometric patterns, colors, and flowing imagery behind closed eyelids. (Greenwood & Skipper 2024 — flicker produces hallucinations ~3x faster than static Ganzfeld.)

Audio pulses complement the visual flicker for stronger combined entrainment. (Tsai lab / MIT GENUS — multimodal stimulation outperforms unimodal.)

Frequency

Flicker rate in cycles per second, constrained to the effective range for photic driving:

  • Theta (4–8 Hz) — Meditation, hypnagogia, creativity. Works via sub-harmonic coupling to alpha.
  • Alpha (8–13 Hz) — Relaxed awareness. The sweet spot. Strongest, most reliable entrainment. 10 Hz is the classic target and peak for geometric visuals. (Frontiers 2016, Rule et al. 2011)
  • Low Beta (13–15 Hz) — Alert focus, finer patterns. Diminishing returns above alpha.

The range is capped at 15 Hz. Below 4 Hz, photic driving is ineffective (~3% response). Above 15 Hz, entrainment weakens and seizure risk peaks at 16 Hz (~90% of photosensitive individuals). (IPS meta-analysis; international photosensitivity guidelines.)

Waveform

  • Sine — Smooth, gentle transitions. Easiest on the eyes. Good for first sessions.
  • Triangle — Linear ramp up/down. Middle ground.
  • Square — Sharp on/off flashes. ~35% stronger cortical response than sine (Zhu et al. 2010). 30% duty cycle locked — optimal for hallucination induction (Amaya et al. 2023).

Audio

A 220 Hz isochronic tone amplitude-modulated at the flicker frequency — the strongest single-method auditory entrainment. (Ioannou et al. 2024; low carriers produce ~3x stronger ASSR — Ross et al. 2003; square AM envelope ~50% more effective — Ferraro et al. 2025.)

The audio envelope is synced to the visual waveform for maximum multisensory coherence.

Descent

The built-in journey: square wave stepping from 12→4 Hz in 0.5 Hz increments. Each step holds steady for the brain to entrain before dropping. Starts in upper alpha, passes through the 10 Hz sweet spot, then descends through theta to the edge of delta. Default 20 minutes.

Upload an audio file (♫) to score the descent — the sweep syncs to your track's length. The first 60 seconds fade in: your music plays immediately while the strobe and tone gently emerge from black/silence, so the soundtrack leads.

Safety

Flickering light can trigger seizures in photosensitive individuals (~1 in 4,000 people). This app is capped at 15 Hz to stay below the peak risk zone (16–20 Hz). Uses white/amber light — saturated red light at any flicker rate is more provocative and is avoided. If you feel any discomfort, stop immediately.
