Photosensitivity Warning

This application produces rapidly flashing lights designed to simulate the Brion Gysin Dream Machine — a stroboscopic device for visual brainwave entrainment.

Do not use if you:

  • Have epilepsy or a history of seizures
  • Have a family history of epilepsy
  • Are prone to photosensitive reactions

For best results: dark room, eyes closed, face toward screen. Headphones or speakers for audio entrainment.

How It Works

The Dream Machine uses flickering light to synchronize your brainwaves through photic driving. Close your eyes, face the screen in a dark room, and let the light pulse through your eyelids. Your visual cortex responds by generating geometric patterns, colors, and flowing imagery.

Pair with headphones for audio-visual entrainment — sound pulses that complement the visual flicker for a stronger combined effect.

Frequency

Flicker rate in cycles per second, targeting different brainwave states:

  • Delta (1–4 Hz) — Deep sleep, unconscious states
  • Theta (4–8 Hz) — Meditation, hypnagogia, creativity
  • Alpha (8–13 Hz) — Relaxed awareness. Sweet spot for geometric visuals. 10 Hz is the classic target.
  • Beta (13–25 Hz) — Alert focus, finer-grained patterns

Higher flicker frequencies produce finer, more detailed hallucinated patterns — the geometry is generated by your cortex, not the screen. (Rule et al. 2011, Heller 2025.)

Spatial Seed

A uniform flickering field is used — your visual cortex generates its own geometric patterns. Produces hallucinations ~3x faster than static Ganzfeld (Greenwood & Skipper 2024).

Optional spatial seeds add a very faint (~5-8%) luminance modulation that can bias which cortical pattern stabilizes. (Rule et al. 2011 — small spatial seeds stabilize specific hallucination forms.)

  • No Seed — Pure uniform field. Patterns emerge spontaneously.
  • Radial — Faint spokes. Biases toward target/bullseye patterns.
  • Spiral — Faint logarithmic spiral. Biases toward spiral hallucinations.
  • Rings — Faint concentric rings. Biases toward tunnel/ring patterns.

Morph

Toggle Morph (or press M) for a hands-free session. All parameters evolve slowly and continuously:

  • Frequency — drifts across delta through beta (2–22 Hz) with layered oscillators (~2.5 min primary cycle)
  • Seed — crossfades smoothly between off, radial, spiral, and rings (~4 min cycle)
  • Color — full spectrum hue rotation — warm ambers, cool blues, soft greens, pale violets (~5 min cycle)
  • Waveform — visual flicker cycles sine → triangle → square (~2 min per phase)
  • Audio mode — cycles tone → AM noise → both (~3 min per phase)
  • Duty cycle — drifts 15–55% during square wave phases
  • Depth — subtle breathing between 70–100%

Touch any manual control to exit morph and take over.

Waveform

  • Sine — Smooth, gentle transitions. Easier on the eyes.
  • Square — Sharp on/off. ~35% stronger cortical response than sine (Zhu et al. 2010). Use with duty cycle control.
  • Triangle — Linear ramp up/down. Middle ground.

Duty Cycle

(Square wave only.) The fraction of each cycle spent bright. 30% is optimal for hallucination induction — brief bright flashes with longer dark periods. (Amaya et al. 2023 used 30% across all conditions.)

Audio

Audio always uses a square envelope (sharp on/off) for maximum entrainment — up to 50% stronger cortical response than sine envelopes. (Ferraro et al. 2025.)

  • Tone — Isochronal tone: 220 Hz carrier amplitude-modulated at the target frequency. The strongest single-method auditory entrainment. (Ioannou et al. 2024; low carriers produce ~3x stronger ASSR — Ross et al. 2003.)
  • AM Noise — Pink noise amplitude-modulated at the target frequency. More pleasant for long sessions; the noise itself becomes the entrainment vehicle.
  • Both — Combined tone + AM noise for layered entrainment.

Noise slider adds unmodulated background pink noise. Default off — background noise can mask the entrainment signal (Ingendoh et al. 2023). Raise only for comfort if needed.

Safety

Flicker can trigger seizures in photosensitive individuals (~1 in 4,000 people). The 15–25 Hz range carries the highest risk. Deep red light (660–780nm) is most provocative — this app uses amber/white. Capped at 25 Hz. If you feel any discomfort, stop immediately.
